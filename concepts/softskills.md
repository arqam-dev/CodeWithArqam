## Behavioral Interview

    - Question 1: Tell me about a time when you had to work with a difficult team member

Answer:

- Project Name: Centraleyes

- Company Name: Tkxel

- Challenge: Team member had multiple commitments, affecting focus and delivery quality

- Approach: 1-on-1 meeting, listened to their workload concerns

- Solution: Assigned separate independent tasks, set clear priorities, established check-ins

- Result: Improved focus, better quality deliverables, project completed on time

- Learned: Proactive communication and clear expectations prevent issues

    - Question 2: Describe a situation where you had to learn a new technology quickly

Answer:

- Project Name: SoftCircles

- Company Name: Abloh

- Challenge: Client requested React JS integration in 2 weeks for new dashboard feature

- Approach: React docs + YouTube tutorials + built small components first

- Solution: Created reusable components, integrated with existing API, used React Router

- Result: Delivered responsive React frontend, client was impressed with performance

- Learned: Always allocate buffer time for new tech, start with small components

<expand title="Leadership & Team Management">
## Leadership & Team Management

    - Question 1: If a person in your team is very intelligent but having behavioral issues, how will you treat them?

Answer:

- Project Name: Centraleyes

- Company Name: Tkxel

- Challenge: Intelligent team member with behavioral issues

- Approach: Step 1: Address in team scrum without pointing, Step 2: Private 1-on-1, Step 3: Written warning, Step 4: HR involvement

- Solution: Understand root cause, offer support, set clear boundaries, document progress

- Result: Improved behavior while retaining talent, or final decision if no improvement

- Learned: Progressive approach preserves talent while maintaining team standards

    - Question 2: If there are 4 days of tasks but client asks to deliver in 1 day, what will you do?

Answer:

- Project Name: Epilogue

- Company Name: Tkxel

- Challenge: 4 days work requested in 1 day

- Approach: Assess what's possible, communicate realistic timeline. Will follow 80/20 principal. 20% effort and 80% impact.

- Solution: Negotiate timeline with client, propose adding extra resources to meet deadline.

- Result: Client agreed to add extra resources, delivered on time with quality maintained

- Learned: Better to under-promise and over-deliver

<expand title="Brain Hacks | How to train your brain">
## Brain Hacks | How to train your brain

    - Learning Myths Busted:
        - Myth 1: You can only learn according to your specific learning style.
        - Myth 2: You can multitask when learning.
        - Myth 3: Testing is a poor tool for learning.
    - Learn with a Friend:
        - Social learning
    - Tell a Story to Learn
    - Manage Distraction:
        - Identify potential distractions and then actions to neutralize them.
    - Sleep to Learn:
        - 7 to 9 hours of sleep daily
        - Room temperature - 18 degree
        - Avoid digital devices before bed
    - Reflection: What Squares:
        - Reflective Learning Principle
        - Generative Learning Principle
        - Reflect what you learned - means revise and generate notes in your own words
    - Why Brain Hacks work:
        - Working:
            - Intake - Acquire and gather information
            - Encode - Classify, examine, store
            - Retrieve - Use and access
        - Ways to improve:
            - Nutrition - Good
            - Stress - Less
            - Sleep - 8h, deep sleep
            - Exercise - Streching, deep breathing, etc
    - Prime your Brain to Learn:
        - Reletive(Relevance) Learning Principle:
            - What will I do with what I learn?
            - What questions do I have about it before I start?
            - What do I already know about it?
    - Timebox your Learning:
        - Promodoro Technique - Spaced Learning Principle:
            - Focus Learning Hours - 25 min of timer
            - Break - 5 min
    - Nutrition: Fuel to Learn:
        - Focused and Engaged Learning Principle:
            - Brain uses 20% to 30% of daily caloric needs
            - Glucose - essential component for brain metabolism
            - Healthy Eating = Healthy Brain
    - Stress Less, Learn More:
        - Take a deep breath
        - Meditate
        - Take a walk
        - Stretch
        - Call a friend
    - Feedback and Learning
    - Move to Learn:
        - Do not sit for hours and hours continously
    - Use Questions to Learn:
        - Write questions without thinking answers. Then after some time or days, go for answering them
    - Making Learning a Habit:
        - Create a time to spend everyday for learning something new
        - Habit Loop: Belief -> Cue -> Act -> Reward
    - Learn through Analogies:
        - Contextual or Generative Learning Principle
        - Phrases for analogy(comparison):
            - This is like ...
            - This reminds me of ...
    - Be a good Strory Listerner:
        - Find the WHY: why speaker is telling this story. concept and problems in story.
        - Personalize the sotry: compare with your own story
        - Retell the story: rephrase in your own words
    - Exercise to Learn
    - Learning with Flashcards
    - Handwritten or Typed Notes
    - Believe to Learn better
    - Get Musical to Get Learning
    - Teach to Learn: The Protege Effect
    - Power Up your Practice
    - Reading is Learning Magic
    - Journaling is Brain Happiness
    - Playable Learning
    - Sentence Starters
    - Mindful Learning
    - "Unlearn" to Learn
